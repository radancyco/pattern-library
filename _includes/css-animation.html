Responsible Animation

Be mindful of the impact that animation can have on people with disabilities. Animation can be a distraction for some, make others ill, and dangerous for those subject to seizures. By adhering to the following rules, we can make our web experiences pleasant and safe for everybody!

  • If animation loops continuously, such as a hero banner video, background animation, auto-scrolling carousel, etc. then it must include a pause button. Third-party plugins, such as Slick, typically have their own implementation of pause.
  • Micro-animations, such as those which occur when scrolling or interacting with page elements, are exempt from having a pause button if they do not last for more than 5 seconds.
  • Developers should honor user settings and implement the prefers-reduced-motion media query to reduce or eliminate animation where needed. See MDN for more details.
  • Animations that flash for more than 3 times per second are prohibited on our websites, as they may induce seizures. A pause button will be ineffective here. If you suspect an animation of having a high flash rate, please bring this to the attention of the Accessibility Team.
