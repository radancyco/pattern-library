Always Link Your Headings

Instead of making your "learn more" text a hyperlink (which would require visually hidden text) or wrapping your entire content block in a hyperlink (which would create additional noise for assistive technology users), make your unique heading the link and then use CSS to allow the entire region to be clickable (a.k.a the "pseudo-element trick"). Also, make sure your heading levels are correct based on surrounding content. Skiping of headings should be avoided.

No Content Means No Heading

When a related content area has no content other than a title, please do not use a heading. Headings should only be used to precede content (paragraphs, lists, etc.). The "pseudo-element trick" can be used here as well, to make entire region clickable.
